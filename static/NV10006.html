NEVADA

The Grass Valley allotment spans 282,854 acres

Allotment health and management

As of 2013, the most recent year of available data, this grazing allotment did not meet rangeland health standards in its last evaluation. Livestock grazing was cited as a factor.

The BLM has no listed allotment management plan, which would set long-term goals for rangeland improvement and outline specific monitoring plans.

Who uses this allotment

Four ranches use this land. Of the four permits issued for this allotment, all four were renewed under a provision in federal law that exempts permits from environmental review.

  • Grass Valley Ranch, Llc based in Austin, NV grazes up to 1,047 cattle between March and November and 6 cattle between April and May
  • Mcclusky Creek Llc. based in Crescent Valley, NV grazes up to 349 cattle between April and January, 146 cattle between July and January, 6 cattle in April and 2 cattle in January
  • University Of Nevada based in Beowawe, NV grazes up to 40 cattle in January and 370 cattle between April and January
  • Dry Creek Ranch based in Austin, NV grazes up to 10 horses between June and August, 244 cattle between June and August
