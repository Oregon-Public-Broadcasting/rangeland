The Duck Creek allotment spans 13,090 acres

Allotment health and management

As of 2013, the most recent year of available data, this grazing allotment did not meet rangeland health standards in its last evaluation. Livestock grazing was cited as a factor.

The BLM has no listed allotment management plan, which would set long-term goals for rangeland improvement and outline specific monitoring plans.

Who uses this allotment

Seven ranches use this land. Of the seven permits issued for this allotment, two were renewed under a provision in federal law that exempts permits from environmental review.

  • B. Reed Groll based in Randolph, UT grazes up to 5 horses between May and September, 65 sheep between May and June, 125 sheep in October and 82 cattle between May and September
  • Peart Ranch Llc based in Randolph, UT grazes up to 3 horses between May and September, 110 cattle between May and September
  • Linda L. Willis based in Laketown, UT grazes up to 51 cattle between May and September
  • Terry Willis based in Laketown, UT grazes up to 74 cattle between May and September
  • Clark Willis based in Logan, UT grazes up to 575 sheep between May and July and 575 sheep between September and December
  • Groll Land & Livestock (Kennedy Lease) based in Randolph, UT grazes up to 6 horses between July and September and 28 cattle between May and September
  • Martin Feller (Kennedy Lease) based in Randolph, UT grazes up to 55 cattle between May and September
