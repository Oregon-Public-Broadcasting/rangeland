The Lower Cattle allotment spans 62,884 acres

Allotment health and management

As of 2013, the most recent year of available data, this grazing allotment did not meet rangeland health standards in its last evaluation. Livestock grazing was cited as a factor.

The BLM has no listed allotment management plan, which would set long-term goals for rangeland improvement and outline specific monitoring plans.

Who uses this allotment

Five ranches use this land. Of the five permits issued for this allotment, all five were renewed under a provision in federal law that exempts permits from environmental review.

  • Gleave, Randy & Family based in Kingston, UT grazes up to 164 cattle between October and April
  • Spencer, T. Samuel based in Escalante, UT grazes up to 77 cattle between October and April
  • Allen, Gary & Family based in Junction, UT grazes up to 185 cattle between October and April
  • Phillips Family, Todd based in Escalante, UT grazes up to 628 cattle between October and April
  • Allen, Scott based in Angle, UT grazes up to 100 cattle between October and April
