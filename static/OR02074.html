OREGON/WASHINGTON

The Pritchard Creek allotment spans 13,587 acres

Allotment health and management

As of 2013, the most recent year of available data, this grazing allotment did not meet rangeland health standards in its last evaluation. Livestock grazing was cited as a factor.

The BLM has no listed allotment management plan, which would set long-term goals for rangeland improvement and outline specific monitoring plans.

Who uses this allotment

Three ranches use this land. Of the three permits issued for this allotment, none were renewed under a provision in federal law that exempts permits from environmental review.

  • M.R. King Ranches Inc. based in Durkee, OR grazes up to 135 cattle between April and May, 238 cattle between November and December, 13 cattle between April and May,
  • Jacobs Ranches Inc. based in Baker City, OR grazes up to 825 sheep in May and 983 sheep in May
  • Wayne & Helen Troy Family Trust Lease based in Baker City, OR grazes up to 300 cattle between October and December and 285 cattle between April and May
